About Me

Hello! I'm Hebba Mohmand, a third-year Computer Engineering student at the University of Virginia, pursuing a focus path in CHIPS — Circuit Hardware Integration for Processing and Systems.

My academic and technical interests lie in electronics, circuit design, hardware-software integration, embedded systems, and using technical solutions in the Finance world (FinTech). Whether it’s building an audio analyzer PCB, writing low-level C code, or building a web app to calculate price totals and discounts, I enjoy applying engineering principles to real-world challenges in creative and impactful ways.

Outside of the classroom, I serve as the Treasurer of the Taiwanese Student Association (TSA) at UVA, where I manage financial records, streamline all budgeting processes, and collaborate with local businesses through our EasyCard initiative, a student perks program inspired by Taiwan’s original EasyCard system. I also work with national and global companies to sponsor our flagship cultural event, Night Market, in partnership with UVA Student Council, the Honor Committee, University Programs Council (UPC), and fellow student cultural organizations across Grounds.

In Fall 2024, I will be joining the UVA School of Engineering as a Grader and Teaching Assistant for ECE 2600 (Electronics), helping students develop a strong foundation in analog circuit design. I also continue to serve as an advisor for the Peer Advising Family Network (PAFN), which supports the transition and success of first-year students in the Asian Pacific Islander South Asian American (APISAA) community at UVA.

Since January 2024, I’ve also been working as a tutor with America Reads, providing academic support and literacy assistance to young students in local Charlottesville schools. Additionally, I volunteer with NOVA RAFT (Resettling Afghan Families Together), assisting with the resettlement of Afghan refugee families in the Northern Virginia area — an experience that’s deepened my commitment to community care and cross-cultural engagement.

Outside of engineering, I enjoy journaling, cooking, graphic design, video editing with Adobe Creative Cloud, exploring local hiking trails and literature, and finding creative ways to build community through storytelling and cultural programming.

If you'd like to see some of my work, feel free to explore my projects or get in touch.
